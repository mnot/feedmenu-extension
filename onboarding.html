Welcome to Feed Menu

Follow your favorite sites without checking back — new posts come to you. Feed Menu finds the feeds a site offers, so you can subscribe in one click.

What's a feed?

A feed is a steady stream of a site's new posts that you read in a feed reader app. Instead of visiting a dozen sites to see what's new, your reader gathers everything in one place. You'll need a feed reader installed to open the feeds that Feed Menu finds.

To get started, pin Feed Menu to your toolbar:

1
Click the Extensions icon (puzzle piece) in the top right of your browser.
2
Find Feed Menu and click the Pin icon.

To get started, pin Feed Menu to your toolbar:

1
Click the Extensions icon (puzzle piece) in your toolbar.
2
Next to Feed Menu, click the gear and choose Pin to Toolbar.

To get started, turn Feed Menu on:

1
Open Safari → Settings → Extensions and switch on Feed Menu. Its icon then appears in your toolbar.
2
Click the Feed Menu icon on any site. The first time, Safari asks to allow access — choose Allow to see that site's feeds.
The Feed Menu icon is always one click away. Open any site and click it to see the feeds you can follow. You can also let the icon light up on its own when a site offers feeds — turn that on anytime from the gear (⚙︎).
